El Cullerot de Sants

Carrer d'Olzinelles 114 · Sants-Montjuïc, 08014 Barcelona

Half past eleven is not lunch in Catalonia — it is esmorzar de forquilla, the fork-and-knife breakfast that Sunday riders have been eating for a century. Heavy, slow-cooked, unfashionable, and exactly the right way to start a day on a bike.

El Cullerot is a tiny neighbourhood room in La Bordeta with the menu chalked up in Catalan only — which is the local shorthand for this place is not for you if you're here for three days. Roughly seventy euros for four, all in.

Order this Cap i pota, or the peus de porc if they have them. The weekend menu costs a couple of euros more precisely because the heavy dishes only appear Saturday and Sunday.
Non-negotiable It seats almost nobody and it is always full. Call and book, or you will be starting your day standing on the pavement.

The Cliff Road

C-31 · Castelldefels → Garraf → Sitges · no toll, no hurry

The corniche. Eighty-six corners cut into the rock with the Mediterranean straight down on your left and nothing between you and it but a low wall. The fast toll road runs inland; everybody sensible pays the toll, which is why this one stays empty of trucks.

Ride it calm — this matters The RACC puts the C-31 among Catalonia's ten most dangerous roads, and motorcyclists are in the majority of its serious accidents. On a September Sunday it is also thick with road cyclists. Single carriageway, no shoulder, blind rock corners. Enjoy the view; do not chase the corner.

Mirador de Cala Morisca

C-31, km 169.5 · Garraf massif, above the water

Most of the pull-offs along this stretch are informal scrapes in the gravel on a blind corner. This one is an actual parking area, which is why it's the only viewpoint in this roadbook. Clifftop, open sea, the coast running away south toward Tarragona.

Fifteen minutes. Helmet off, look at it properly, get back on.

El Chiringuito de Garraf

Avinguda Llorach 5 · 08871 Garraf

Garraf is nine hundred people, a rank of striped 1920s bathing huts, and a beach the train stops at. The chiringuito sits on the sand with a proper motorcycle area beside the old beach tunnel, which tells you exactly who its customers are.

A cortado, maybe a vermut if you're not the one riding fastest. Boots off, feet in the sand. This is the halfway pause, not a meal — you have a monastery to get to.

Sitges, the back streets

Carrer Major & Carrer d'en Bosch · behind the Sant Bartomeu church

Sitges has a seafront promenade that belongs to the cruise crowd, and a knot of old streets behind the church that doesn't. Park the bikes, walk away from the water, and stay in the lanes around Carrer Major.

Sitges holds a llibertat d'horaris exemption, so unlike most of Catalonia the shops and bars are genuinely open on a Sunday afternoon. Forty minutes. Don't sit down for a meal here — dinner is worth waiting for.

Sakya Tashi Ling monastery

Palau Novella, Plana Novella · Olivella, inside the Parc del Garraf · 41°17′30″N 1°51′09″E

An 1890 Catalan modernist mansion, marooned in a limestone valley in the middle of a natural park, bought by a Buddhist community in 1996 and turned into a working monastery. The road in — the BV-2111 up through Plana Novella — is worth the ticket on its own.

Nobody stumbles onto this. It is the strangest thing you will see all day and it is forty minutes from a beach bar.

Phone ahead — sources disagree The monastery's own site lists Sunday 10:00–18:00 with guided tours at 11:00, 12:00, 13:00 and 16:30 (€9 / €7); the Catalan press lists a longer day and more tours. It also closes without notice for retreats. Ring +34 93 743 20 10 on Saturday. The 16:30 tour is the one that fits.

Over the back of the massif

BV-2411 · Olesa de Bonesvalls → Gavà, then the C-31 north into the city

Rather than double back down the coast road you've already ridden, drop over the inland side of the Garraf. Open plateau, pine, almost no traffic, and a long descent into Gavà. This is the road Barcelona riders name when you ask them where they go after work.

You come into the city from the south-west, past the port, and pick up the water at Barceloneta — which is exactly where you want to be at half past six.

Jon Cake Poblenou

Carrer de Lope de Vega 93 · Poblenou · their largest shop, ~350 m²

Jon García trained at El Celler de Can Roca, quit aeronautical engineering at twenty-seven, and now makes the cheesecake the whole city queues for. Baked that morning, sold by weight, a rotating flavour every fortnight alongside the classic.

Take the Poblenou branch, not the Born one — the Born shop has a queue down the street and you'd be parking a motorcycle in Ciutat Vella's restricted zone. Poblenou is on the Cerdà grid, five minutes off the seafront, and you're going to be eating dinner three streets away.

Order this The classic, and whatever the two-week flavour is. Ask for it warm.
Two things to know The Poblenou hours (08:00–20:00 daily) come from press coverage of the opening, not from a live listing — check the day before. If it's shut, the Santa Caterina shop at Carrer de Sant Pere Més Baix 36 is officially open daily 10:00–20:00 and takes the same detour.

The seafront run

Joan de Borbó → Passeig Marítim (upper) → Avinguda del Litoral → Fòrum · ~8.5 km

The whole day has been pointing at this. Start at Passeig de Joan de Borbó with the marina on your right, climb onto the upper vehicle level of the Passeig Marítim past the Hospital del Mar, and then run Avinguda del Litoral east with Bogatell, Mar Bella and Nova Mar Bella opening up beside you.

Park at Plaça del Fòrum — there are dedicated motorcycle bays by the CCIB — and walk out to the Zona de Banys for the last of it. Sunset is at 20:20. Then turn around and ride back into Poblenou with the light behind you.

Ride the avenue, not the promenade The beachfront promenades at Bogatell and Mar Bella are pedestrian and cycle only — Avinguda del Litoral, one block back, is your legal line. And don't take the Ronda Litoral (B-10) by mistake: it's the elevated ring road, perfectly legal and completely pointless for this.

Els Pescadors

Plaça de Prim 1 · Poblenou · a fishermen's square under old ombú trees

Forty years ago this was a neighbourhood bar on a cobbled square in the old fishing quarter. It is now the seafood restaurant that Barcelona keeps for itself — no picture menus, no Port Vell, no one outside trying to walk you in.

The square is a hundred metres across, lit under two enormous trees, and the ride finishes with everyone sitting at one table in it. Reckon on €55–70 a head with wine. The kitchen shuts at 22:15, so 20:45 is the right hour, not a suggestion.

Order this An arròs negre or the fideuà for the table. Gambes de Palamós if they're in season — early September, they should be. Suquet de peix if the table wants a stew.
Reserve today A table for six on a September Sunday will not exist if you leave it to the week of. This is the single booking that decides whether the day ends well.

Rider Notes

Read before Sunday

The C-31 deserves respect

The RACC ranks the Garraf coastal stretch among the ten most dangerous roads in Catalonia, with roughly six fatal or serious accidents a year — and motorcyclists make up the majority of them. Narrow, unshouldered, continuous no-overtaking lines.

On a Sunday morning it is full of road cyclists. Ride it as a scenic road, sit behind the group, and take your pictures at Cala Morisca where there's a real parking area rather than on a blind curve.

The ZBE does not apply

Barcelona's low-emission zone only restricts motorcycles and mopeds registered before roughly 2003, and enforcement runs Monday to Friday, 07:00–20:00 only. A Sunday ride is outside the window entirely, whatever you're on. No sticker, no problem.

Separately: Carretera de les Aigües is closed to motorcycles — Collserola park ordinance, article 14. It isn't on this route, but people keep suggesting it.

Where you actually park

Free white-lined motorcycle bays exist across the city. On this route: Passeig Marítim near the Hospital del Mar, and Plaça del Fòrum by the CCIB, both with dedicated moto spaces.

Avoid taking bikes deep into Ciutat Vella — it's a restricted-circulation zone, which is the practical reason this roadbook sends you to Jon Cake's Poblenou shop instead of the Born original.

Before you go — the checklist

  • Book Els Pescadors for 20:45, six people. Do it first.
  • Book El Cullerot de Sants for 11:30. It's tiny.
  • Ring the monastery Saturday — +34 93 743 20 10 — it closes for retreats without notice.
  • Check Jon Cake Poblenou is open. Their published hours aren't on a live listing.
  • Fuel up before Sitges. The Garraf interior is thin on stations.
  • Sunset 20:20 — leave the Fòrum by 20:30 to make dinner.
