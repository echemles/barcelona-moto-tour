El Cullerot de Sants

Carrer d'Olzinelles 114 · Sants-Montjuïc, 08014 · 934 31 90 69

Half past eleven is not lunch in Catalonia. It is esmorzar de forquilla — the fork-and-knife breakfast that riders have been eating before a Sunday run for a century. Heavy, slow-cooked, unfashionable, and the correct way to start a day on a bike.

El Cullerot is a small room in La Bordeta with the board chalked up in Catalan only, which is the local shorthand for this place is not for people who are here for three days. It also sits at the foot of Montjuïc, which is where you're going next.

Order this Cap i pota, or the peus de porc if they have them. The weekend menu costs a couple of euros more precisely because the heavy dishes only appear on Saturday and Sunday.
Book it, and have a backup It seats almost nobody and it is always full. Its Sunday hours are consistent across listings but I couldn't confirm them against a primary source — so if the phone doesn't answer, Ca la Montse in Barceloneta (Sun 10:00–16:00) is confirmed on its own site and takes bookings online.

The road Circuit de Montjuïc · 1969–1975

The old circuit

Avinguda de l'Estadi → Avinguda de Miramar · Montjuïc

Four Formula One Grands Prix and decades of motorcycle racing ran on these public roads. The Recta de l'Estadi was the pit straight — it runs along the flank of the Olympic stadium above. The Revolt de Miramar is still there, still off-camber, still with the whole port opening up underneath it as you come out.

Nobody has raced here since 1975 and there is no plaque worth stopping for. You just ride Avinguda de l'Estadi, drop round onto Miramar, and know what you're on. Do it twice. Then do it once more, slowly, and look down at the water instead.

Two caveats, honestly Passeig Olímpic and Avinguda dels Montanyans get called "the circuit" online — neither was part of the 1969–75 lap, so don't chase a perfect reconstruction. And Montjuïc's roads close for events at the stadium. No standing Sunday closure exists, but check the event calendar the week before.

Cactus, and the whole port

Jardins de Mossèn Costa i Llobera + Mirador de l'Alcalde · Montjuïc

One of Europe's great cactus collections, planted on a south-facing slope above the container cranes, free to walk into, and almost always empty because everybody queues for the castle two hundred metres uphill instead.

Walk it, then stand at the Mirador de l'Alcalde and look at the port working on a Sunday. Free moto parking along Carrer dels Tarongers and the Passeig del Migdia.

Skip the castle The old "free on Sunday afternoons" rule doesn't appear anywhere in the Castell's current 2026 admissions sheet — you'd pay full price to join a queue. The gardens are better and cost nothing.

Up to Vallvidrera

Avinguda del Tibidabo → Carretera de Vallvidrera al Tibidabo · descend by the Arrabassada

Barcelona's own mountain road, still inside the city boundary. Hairpins under the pines, the whole grid laid out below you through the gaps, and a 125 is genuinely the right tool for it — you'll be in the fat of the powerband where a big bike is just short-shifting.

Stop in Vallvidrera village — it feels like a hill town that Barcelona happened to grow underneath. Antiga Casa Trampa, on the plaça, has been there since 1882 and is open Sunday until 17:00. Come down the Carretera de l'Arrabassada.

This leg may not exist on the day Collserola's natural environment has been closed since March 2026 for African swine fever, with no reopening date announced. Paved through-roads are exempt, so the climb itself should be legal — but trails and forest access are not, and the situation is live. Check parcnaturalcollserola.cat the week before. If it's shut, skip straight to vermut and give Montjuïc the extra hour.
Carretera de les Aigües is banned Everyone recommends it. It is closed to motor vehicles — Collserola park ordinance article 14, and named again under the swine-fever order. Don't.

Bar Rovira

Carrer del Clot 41 · El Clot, Sant Martí · 93 245 10 62 · family-run since 1963

The vermut hour is the most Catalan thing that happens on a Sunday, and it has been comprehensively colonised in Gràcia and on Passeig de Sant Joan. El Clot has not been. Bar Rovira has been in the same family for sixty years and the local press keeps writing pieces that amount to go here instead.

A vermut with the soda and the olive, sitting on a terrace on an ordinary street where nothing is happening. This is the pause in the middle of the day, and the whole reason not to leave the city.

One unresolved detail Everything confirms they're open Sunday; nothing agrees on when they close. One listing implies terrace service only. Ring ahead if you're arriving late.

Jon Cake Poblenou

Carrer de Lope de Vega 93 · Poblenou · their largest shop, ~350 m²

Jon García trained at El Celler de Can Roca, quit aeronautical engineering at twenty-seven, and now makes the cheesecake the whole city queues for. Baked that morning, sold by weight, a new flavour every fortnight beside the classic.

Take Poblenou, not the Born original — the Born shop has a queue down the street and you'd be trying to park a bike in Ciutat Vella's restricted zone. Poblenou is on the open grid, five minutes from the water, and dinner is three streets away.

Order this The classic, and whatever the two-week flavour is. Ask for it warm.
Ring the day before The Poblenou hours (08:00–20:00 daily) trace back to a single press piece from the opening, not a live listing. The Santa Caterina shop at Sant Pere Més Baix 36 has Sunday 10:00–20:00 corroborated across four independent listings — it's the safer bet if you can't confirm, though you'll be walking in from a moto bay outside the old town.

Feet in the Barceloneta

Passeig de Joan de Borbó · park in the white-line bays, walk to the sand

Ride down Joan de Borbó with the fishing marina on your right — stay out of the bus lanes, motorcycles have been banned from them here since 2011. Park in a free white-line moto bay, walk across, and stand on the beach for half an hour with your boots off.

You're facing east-southeast. The sun is behind you, dropping toward Collserola — toward the road you were riding four hours ago. That's the whole shape of this day, and you can see both ends of it from here.

The seafront run

Joan de Borbó → Avinguda del Litoral → Parc del Fòrum · then back to Plaça de Prim

The whole day has been aimed at this. Pick up Avinguda del Litoral and run east with Bogatell, Mar Bella and Nova Mar Bella opening beside you, the light going long and orange across your right shoulder, the sea ahead turning the colour of the sky.

Sunset is 20:16, due west — behind you, behind Collserola. What you get out here is the afterglow on the water and the whole beachfront in it. Turn at the Fòrum, park among the moto bays by the CCIB if you want the last of it, then double back into Poblenou.

Ride the avenue, not the promenade Passeig Marítim de la Barceloneta is pedestrian now — both levels. So are the Bogatell and Mar Bella promenades. Avinguda del Litoral, one block back, is the legal line, and it's a proper 50 km/h road. Don't confuse it with the Ronda Litoral (B-10), the elevated ring road — legal on a 125, pointless for this.

Els Pescadors

Plaça de Prim 1 · Poblenou · 932 25 20 18 · a fishermen's square under old ombú trees

Forty years ago this was a neighbourhood bar on a cobbled square in the old fishing quarter. It is now the seafood restaurant Barcelona keeps for itself — no picture menus, no Port Vell, nobody outside trying to walk you in.

The square is a hundred metres across, lit under two enormous trees, and the day ends with everyone at one table in the middle of it. Reckon on €55–70 a head with wine. The kitchen stops at 22:15, so 20:45 is the hour, not a suggestion.

Order this An arròs negre or the fideuà for the table. Gambes de Palamós if they're in season — early September, they should be. Suquet de peix if the table wants a stew.
Reserve today A table for six on a September Sunday will not exist if you leave it to the week of. This is the single booking that decides whether the day ends well. Book online through their site, or call 932 25 20 18.

Rider Notes

Read before Sunday

The 125 is not the constraint

Spanish law bars mopeds (≤50 cc) from autopistas and autovías — not 125s. A 125 cc L3e is legal on the Ronda de Dalt and the Ronda Litoral. This route simply never needs them.

The ZBE is enforced Monday to Friday, 07:00–20:00 only, so a Sunday ride is outside the window entirely, whatever the bike's emissions label. And motorcycles are banned from Barcelona's bus and taxi lanes — which matters on Joan de Borbó, where there's one in each direction.

Where the roads actually aren't

Carretera de les Aigües: closed to motor vehicles. Collserola ordinance article 14, and named again in the swine-fever order. Everyone will recommend it anyway.

Passeig Marítim de la Barceloneta: pedestrian, both levels. So are the Bogatell and Mar Bella promenades. Avinguda del Litoral is the only legal seafront line.

Bunkers del Carmel: deliberately not on this route. Resident-permit parking on the approach streets, gated nightly, and the council spent 2026 trying to regulate the crowds. It's the definition of what you asked me to avoid.

Parking, free and otherwise

The white-line moto bays are free, unreserved, and everywhere — about 95,000 of them citywide, no app, no ticket. Find the nearest at areaverda.cat.

Pavement parking is conditionally legal — over 3 m wide, half a metre off the kerb, 3 m left clear — and getting it wrong runs from €50 to €500. Just use the bays.

Before you go — the checklist

  • Book Els Pescadors for 20:45. Do this one first.
  • Book El Cullerot de Sants for 11:30 — 934 31 90 69. Backup: Ca la Montse.
  • Check Collserola is open — closure notices. Swine fever, closed since March.
  • Check Montjuïc's event calendar for 6 Sept — roads close for stadium events.
  • Confirm Jon Cake Poblenou is open. Their hours aren't on a live listing.
  • Sunset 20:16 — be on Avinguda del Litoral by 19:50.
